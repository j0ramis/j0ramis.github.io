CRAP Assessment of Modern Web Designs

LEGO

A Masterclass in CRAP Principles

LEGO's official online store balances a vibrant, playful identity with clean e-commerce usability, creating an energetic yet highly navigable showcase for their building systems.

  • Contrast: Executed highly effectively by isolating bright, colorful product images against pristine white grid cards and deep neutral backgrounds. Crucial UI cues—like the bright yellow "Add to Bag" buttons or primary red promotional tabs—pop dramatically, letting users locate actions instantly.
  • Repetition: Strongly reinforced throughout the interface via standard product card frameworks, uniform typography scales for age ranges/piece counts, and consistent structural elements like the universal navigation header. This repetitive layout predictable rhythm keeps the browsing experience intuitive across diverse product themes.
  • Alignment: Anchored to a rigid, multi-column grid layout that keeps hundreds of product images, star ratings, and price tags cleanly organized. Text blocks align cleanly to the left margins of their containing boxes, eliminating stray design pieces and establishing a sharp, intentional visual structure.
  • Proximity: Grouped closely together to form logical context packs. Age tags, part metrics, and consumer review scores are nestled tightly underneath their respective product shots, while distinct margins separate individual thematic collections to prevent messy visual bleeding as users scroll.

Arngren

A Masterclass in CRAP Violations

Arngren is a legendary example of early internet chaotic design, presenting an unorganized, overwhelming classified directory layout that completely ignores usability.

  • Contrast: Extremely deficient because dozens of tiny text snippets in random colors (blue, red, yellow, green) are layered on top of pixelated image backgrounds or bright table borders. This makes identifying clickable textual pathways a straining physical effort.
  • Repetition: Completely absent across the interface; animated graphics, irregular HTML border lines, variable font sizes, and mismatched graphical scales fight for user attention at the exact same moment without a cohesive underlying pattern.
  • Alignment: Virtually non-existent on the canvas, resulting in a disorganized mosaic look. Items, product listings, and banners are stuffed into arbitrary locations, forcing visitors to scroll horizontally and vertically just to browse through structural content blocks.
  • Proximity: Entirely violated throughout the index. Price tags are detached from their corresponding electronic products, and completely unrelated items (like small hobby drones and full-sized cars) are packed into compressed grid cells without separating whitespace padding.
